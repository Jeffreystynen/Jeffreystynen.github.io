One of the datasets we delved into was the renowned Titanic dataset. Through this project, I gained hands-on experience in utilizing powerful tools such as Pandas and Seaborn for data cleaning and analysis. Our objective was to derive valuable insights from the dataset, ultimately enabling us to calculate the probability of survival.

In another project, we focused on analyzing a dataset related to crop yields. Our aim was to develop predictive models for forecasting future crop yields. This endeavor involved leveraging technologies including Pandas, AWS, and Seaborn to manipulate and analyze the data effectively.

During this team project we all engaged in the different datasets and models. This allowed us to learn the entire process and compare the results to gain a better understanding of the subject. What I contributed to the final rusult can be found in the titanic notebook in this GitHub repository where I took the liberty to combine all of our ideas together and forge a well structured notbook with a robust model to calculate the probability of survival.
